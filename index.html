About Me

I'm an immunologist and translational scientist with over 20 years of experience bringing cancer vaccines from the bench to the clinic. My work centers on neoantigen discovery, T-cell immunology, and designing immune assays that help us understand whether our vaccines are actually working in patients. I've led the bench-to-clinic translation of personalized DNA and idiotype-based vaccines through Phase I/II clinical trials, coordinating IND-enabling studies across multidisciplinary teams. What drives me is the intersection of deep immunology expertise with emerging computational tools — turning complex multi-omics data into actionable insights for precision oncology.

AI-Assisted Bulk RNA-seq Analysis Reveals Fusion Landscape Remodeling Following Personalized Neoantigen Vaccination in LPL/WM

Using Arriba-based fusion detection on paired pre- and post-vaccination RNA-seq, we identified 35 high-confidence gene fusions with striking post-treatment enrichment. Key findings include BCL2L11 disruption (apoptosis resistance), SF1-SF3B1 splicing factor fusion, and CIITA rearrangement (immune evasion) — all emerging exclusively after vaccination.
