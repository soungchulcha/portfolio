6. Manuscript Text

Methods

To characterize vaccine-induced changes in T-cell clonal composition, we analyzed matched single-cell TCR-seq data from paired pre- and post-vaccination bone marrow samples of 7 LPL/WM patients. For each patient, the 20 most prevalent TCR clonotypes were identified from the post-vaccination sample based on TRB CDR3 amino acid sequence frequency. The frequency of each clonotype was calculated as the proportion of TCR-positive T cells carrying that clonotype relative to the total number of TCR-positive T cells in the corresponding patient-timepoint sample. The matched pre-vaccination frequencies for these same 20 clonotypes were then retrieved; clonotypes absent in the pre-vaccination sample were assigned a frequency of zero and flagged as newly emerged. A two-sided paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test was performed per patient to compare pre- and post-vaccination frequencies of the selected 20 clonotypes.

Results

Analysis of the 20 most prevalent TCR clonotypes detected post-vaccination revealed significant clonal expansion in 5 of 7 patients (71%; paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test, p < 0.05). Patient 3 showed the strongest response (p = 6.2 × 10−5), with 18 of 20 top post-vaccination clonotypes newly emerged and absent in the pre-vaccination sample. Patients 1, 4, 2, and 7 similarly demonstrated significant expansion of post-vaccination clonotypes (p = 7.3 × 10−4 to 0.010), with 3 to 13 newly emerged clonotypes per patient. In contrast, Patients 5 and 9 did not show significant changes (p = 0.185 and p = 0.304, respectively), with the majority of their top post-vaccination clonotypes already detectable at comparable frequencies before vaccination. Across all patients, a total of 60 newly emerged clonotypes were identified among the 140 top post-vaccination clonotypes analyzed (42.9%), consistent with vaccine-induced de novo T-cell clonal expansion. These findings are concordant with Kwak et al. (2024), who reported that the majority of the 20 most prevalent post-vaccination clonotypes increased from low-frequency pre-existing clones, with newly emerging clonotypes comprising approximately 4.4% of the total repertoire.

Reproducibility Note

Input files: TCR-unpaired-clonotypes.csv from 10x Genomics VDJ pipeline (LPL_BCR_TCR/ folder)

Clonotype definition: TRB CDR3 amino acid sequence (cdr3s column, chain == "TRB")

Frequency denominator: Total TCR-positive T cells per patient-timepoint (sum of all TRB clonotype cell counts)

Top 20 selection: From POST sample only, ranked by cell count (frequency column)

Statistical test: Two-sided paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test per patient

Patients: 7 paired (Patient 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 9). Patient 6 excluded (no Post). Patient 8 excluded (no TCR files).
